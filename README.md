# Financial Audit Analytics & Risk Assessment Dashboard
### Client-Based Financial Analysis Case Study | Power BI & Excel


# Project Background

This project was developed using a 3-year financial dataset of a UAE-based logistics and technology business. The objective was to transform raw audit and accounting data into a structured financial analysis dashboard that could support management-level decision-making.

The project focused on analyzing:
* Revenue performance
* Expense behavior
* Profitability
* Liquidity position
* Financial risk exposure

The final solution was delivered through interactive Power BI dashboards supported by business insight reporting.

# Business Problem

The client’s financial data was unstructured and spread across multiple years with inconsistent account classifications. Management required a consolidated view of the company’s financial performance and risk exposure to better understand:

* Whether the business was growing profitably
* Which costs were impacting performance
* How liquidity and receivables were changing
* What financial risks required attention

# Data Preparation & Structuring

The financial statements for 2023 and 2024 were cleaned and standardized in Excel before being imported into Power BI.
The dataset was structured into:

* Revenue
* Cost of Revenue (COGS)
* Operating Expenses
* Other Income/Expenses
* Assets
* Liabilities
* Equity

Additional calculated columns were created for:
* Statement Type (BS / PL)
* Reporting Categories
* Positive/Negative financial presentation
* KPI aggregation

A separate 2025 risk dataset was also created to evaluate changes in:
* Cash balances
* Trade receivables
* Credit risk exposure

# Dashboard 1 — Financial Performance Overview

The first dashboard focused on the company’s financial performance across 2023 and 2024.

## KPI Highlights
### Total Revenue

The business generated strong revenue primarily through:

* Subscription revenue
* Maintenance revenue
* License-based services
Revenue growth improved significantly in 2024, especially due to increased subscription income.

### Total Expenses

Operating expenses remained high across both years due to:

* Royalty expenses
* External affiliated services
* Consulting and management service costs
* Employee-related expenses
The analysis showed that operational growth was accompanied by increasing cost pressure.

###  Net Profit

Although the company remained profitable overall, net profit margins were relatively low compared to total revenue and liabilities.
This indicated that rising operational and financing costs were reducing profitability efficiency.

### Balance Sheet Position

The company maintained:

* Positive total equity
* Strong asset base
* Significant liability exposure
Large related-party liabilities and financing obligations were identified as key balance sheet risks.


# Dashboard 2 — Financial Risk Assessment (2025)
A separate risk dashboard was developed using the 2025 financial risk disclosures.

## Key Risk Findings

### Declining Cash Position

Cash and bank balances decreased from:

* AED 533K (2024)
  to
* AED 338K (2025)

This indicated reduced short-term liquidity availability.

### Rising Trade Receivables
Trade receivables increased from:

* AED 405K (2024)
  to
* AED 658K (2025)

This suggested:
* slower customer collections
* increasing credit exposure
* higher working capital pressure

### Credit Risk Observation
The company became more dependent on customer collections while cash reserves declined.
This increased the company’s exposure to:

* collection delays
* liquidity pressure
* operational cash flow risk

# Key Business Insights

## 1. Revenue Growth Was Positive

The company achieved overall revenue growth across the analysis period, especially in subscription-based services.
## 2. Cost Structure Requires Attention

Royalty expenses, affiliated service costs, and administrative expenses consumed a large portion of revenue.
This reduced overall profitability efficiency.


## 3. Receivable Risk Increased

Trade receivables grew significantly in 2025, indicating slower customer payments and increased collection risk.

## 4. Liquidity Pressure Was Identified

Declining cash balances highlighted potential short-term liquidity concerns.

## 5. Financial Stability Still Maintained

Despite rising risks, the company maintained a positive equity position, indicating overall financial stability.

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were identified:

* Improve customer collection processes
* Strengthen working capital management
* Reduce dependency on high operational service costs
* Monitor related-party liabilities more closely
* Improve liquidity and cash flow planning
* Implement tighter financial risk monitoring procedures
